# Token Revenue Treasury Product Layer

## 1. Purpose

This document explains the Token / Revenue / Treasury product layer after the Fair Launch tokenomics correction.

The key distinction:

- ALPHA launch model: Fair Launch.
- Treasury split model: protocol revenue split after revenue enters Treasury.

The Treasury split is not ALPHA token supply allocation.

## 2. Why ALPHA Exists

ALPHA is planned as the coordination asset for:

- DAO governance participation.
- Protocol coordination.
- Staking participation.
- Ecosystem alignment.
- Risk reporting and dispute governance participation.

ALPHA token launch is pending. Mainnet is not live.

## 3. Fair Launch Boundary

Planned launch model:

- Fair Launch.
- No project-side reserved token allocation.
- No team preallocation.
- No VC allocation.
- No DAO treasury token allocation at launch.
- No staking token reserve at launch.
- No builders / contributors token allocation at launch.
- Market participants acquire ALPHA through the open market after launch.

There is no hidden project-side token reserve.

## 4. Protocol Revenue Flow

Future protocol revenue may come from:

- Green Label fees.
- Protocol services.
- Future creator fees.
- Ecosystem revenue.

Revenue flow:

Protocol Revenue / Project Revenue
-> Treasury
-> 50% Relief Pool
-> 20% Buyback / Burn
-> 20% DAO payroll / builders / contributors
-> 10% Staking Rewards

This is a revenue accounting model, not initial token allocation.

## 5. Treasury V2 Split

The `50 / 20 / 20 / 10` split applies to revenue after it enters Treasury:

- 50% Relief Pool.
- 20% Buyback / Burn.
- 20% DAO payroll / builders / contributors.
- 10% Staking Rewards.

Treasury V2 has been verified on Devnet for USDC split accounting.

## 6. Staking Rewards Are Not Guaranteed Yield

Staking rewards come from the `10%` Treasury revenue split.

They do not come from a preallocated ALPHA staking reserve.

Staking rewards are not:

- Fixed APY.
- Guaranteed yield.
- Guaranteed returns.
- A dividend promise.

Rewards depend on real protocol revenue, pool balance, protocol rules, and future governance.

## 7. Buyback / Burn Is Not Price Support

Buyback / burn comes from `20%` of protocol revenue.

It is not:

- Guaranteed buyback.
- Price floor support.
- Price protection.
- Guaranteed liquidity support.
- A promise of token price appreciation.

Any buyback / burn policy should be transparent, rule-bound, and governance-reviewed.

## 8. Relief Pool Is Not Insurance

The Relief Pool receives `50%` of protocol revenue under the revenue split model.

It may support victim protection, risk response, and future DAO-reviewed relief policy.

The Relief Pool does not mean automatic payout.

Green Label is not insurance and not a credit rating.

## 9. DAO Payroll / Builders / Contributors

`20%` of protocol revenue may support:

- DAO payroll.
- Builders.
- Contributors.
- Protocol maintenance.
- Audits.
- Ecosystem operations.

This is revenue-based, not a preallocated token bucket.

Future spending should be transparent and governance-reviewed.

## 10. DAO Oversight

DAO / Security Layer oversight should govern:

- Treasury policy.
- Builders / contributors spending.
- Relief policy.
- Staking reward policy.
- Buyback / burn execution rules.
- Critical authority changes.

Full ALPHA voting layer is pending.
